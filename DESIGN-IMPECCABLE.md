## 1. Overview

**Creative North Star: "Augmented Mastery"**

The Learning Society is the operating system for Skills-Based Organizations. The learner-practitioner is protagonist of every screen — 80% of surfaces, 100% of time spent. The AI and the coach augment their practice; they never substitute for it. The L&D pilot conducts a portfolio of practitioners — never a dashboard of numbers.

The design system rejects three category reflexes:

- It rejects **Moodle grey** — the bureaucratic LMS energy of dead tables, unsigned forms, and compliance-checkbox dread.
- It rejects **Duolingo bright** — the saturated kindergarten primaries and Pavlovian streak pressure that infantilize the adult learner.
- It rejects **AI SaaS dark-mode purple** — the gradient-glass mood of a tool that promises everything and trusts nothing.

What remains is a warm-light system in service of practice:

- **Coastal teal** carries focus and progress (the learner's path through the parcours, the Dreyfus radar, the lesson player).
- **Warm amber** carries human coaching contact (the coach session, the correction, the messaging thread).
- **Golden sun** marks the moment of practice validated (the JAC ceremony, the badge unlock, the project completion).
- A tightly disciplined grey scale carries everything else.

Glass morphism is a signal, not a finish. Editorial typography brings authority. Pill-shaped CTAs signal commitment. **The system reads as the opposite of a presence sheet.**

### Key Characteristics

- **Tonal system, not multicolor.** Three saturated colors total (teal / amber / sun). Each carries a clear semantic load: focus & progress / human coaching / practice validated. Never decorative.
- **Pill-shaped CTAs.** Every button is `{rounded.pill}` 999px. No square buttons, no `rounded-md`. The pill is the brand's signature gesture of commitment.
- **Generous radii everywhere else.** Cards at `{rounded.xl}` 20px, inputs at `{rounded.lg}` 14px. The system reads as warm and modern, never harsh.
- **League Spartan display + Nunito body.** A condensed editorial display paired with a rounded humanist sans. French editorial sensibility, not generic system-ui.
- **Glass as signal.** `backdrop-blur-glass-medium` (16px) appears only on overlapping surfaces — modals, sticky headers, hero cards over photography. Never as decorative tint on a static card.
- **Warm, tonal shadows.** Hover-state shadows carry the element's tint. The system avoids the cliché neutral drop-shadow.
- **One altitude per viewport.** Operational (learner / coach) or Strategic (manager / CLO) — never both in the same surface. Mixing destroys decision velocity. See §6.
- **Practice grammar is the language.** Every verb privileges active practice (reprends, valide, soumets, maîtrise) over passive consumption (visionne, termine, complète). See §7.
- **AI is transparent instrument.** Every AI output carries a label, source citation, confidence score, and override mechanism. Never magic copilot. See §8.

## 2. Colors

The palette is a tonal trio anchored on coastal teal, with two warm accents and a tightly disciplined neutral scale. Saturation is intentionally lower than the EdTech category default — the system is meant to be lived in for 20-minute sessions, not glanced at in app-store screenshots.

### Primary
- **Coastal Teal** (`#55A1B4`): The system's anchor. Focus rings, brand mark, hover-state surfaces, `card-tinted-primary` ladder. The visual signature of the brand.
- **Coastal Teal CTA** (`#4A8FA1`): One step deeper than the anchor. Default fill of every `button-primary`. Carries actions, not decoration.
- **Coastal Teal Pressed** (`#3D7786`): Active state. Used only on `:active`, never at rest.
- **Teal Darkest** (`#1F3E45`): Hero headlines on dark surfaces, deep-water gradient stops, `card-glass-dark` anchor.
- **Teal Mist** (`#E8F4F7`): Tinted card surfaces, ghost button backgrounds, gentle hover overlays. The lightest teal that still reads as teal.

### Secondary
- **Warm Amber** (`#ED843A`): The coaching color. `button-secondary`, `card-tinted-warm`, coaching session cards, masterclass surfaces. Reserved for warmth and human encounter — never on system controls.
- **Warm Amber Deep** (`#C06920`): Active state. Text color on warm-tinted card surfaces.
- **Warm Amber Mist** (`#FFF3EB`): Tinted surface for coaching sections, masterclass cards.

### Tertiary
- **Golden Sun** (`#F8B044`): The practice-validated color. Badges, XP indicators, JAC ceremony, completion markers. Scarce by doctrine. Never a CTA background, never a section fill.
- **Golden Sun Mist** (`#FFF9EE`): Reserved for achievement and reflection surfaces.

### Neutral
- **Ink Deepest** (`#1a1a1a`): Headings, primary text on light surfaces. Never `#000000`.
- **Ink Body** (`#374151`): Default body copy. Reading-comfort color at 16px Nunito 400.
- **Ink Muted** (`#6b7280`): Metadata, captions, helper text.
- **Ink Subtle** (`#9ca3af`): Placeholders, disabled states, least-emphasis text.
- **Border Neutral** (`#e5e7eb`): Default card and input border.
- **Border Subtle** (`#f3f4f6`): Barely-there dividers between dense content blocks.
- **Surface White** (`#ffffff`): The page canvas.
- **Surface Cool** (`#f9fafb`): Page background on dashboards and dense data surfaces.

### Semantic
- **Success Sage** (`#9DBEBA`): Muted teal-green. Completion states, validated competencies. Doctrinal departure from the bright `#22C55E` success-green of generic SaaS — this is the "calmly validated" tone, not the "you crushed it" tone.
- **Danger Coral** (`#F28559`): Soft coral, not bright red. Errors, warnings, destructive confirmations. Rhymes with the warm-amber family — reads as a flag, not a slap.

### Named Rules

**The Three-Saturated Rule.** Only three saturated colors exist in this system: coastal teal, warm amber, and golden sun. Any saturated color that isn't one of these (`#3B82F6` blue, `#22C55E` green, `#EF4444` red, `#8B5CF6` purple) is forbidden in product surfaces. Semantic states use the tinted muted family (`success-sage`, `danger-coral`) — never the generic Tailwind defaults.

**The One Tone Per Section Rule.** A screen carries a dominant tone and at most one accent. Mixing all three saturated tones with equal weight produces a Christmas-tree page that destroys the editorial calm. See §10 for per-flow tone mapping.

**The Tinted Mist Rule.** Background tints (`teal-mist`, `warm-amber-mist`, `golden-sun-mist`) carry tone without carrying weight. They're used for card surfaces, hero overlays, and section dividers — never for body text background (that's always `surface-white` or `surface-cool`).

## 3. Typography

**Display Font:** League Spartan (with Helvetica Neue fallback)
**Body Font:** Nunito (with -apple-system fallback)
**Mono Font:** JetBrains Mono (reserved for eyebrows, code, micro-labels)

**Character:** League Spartan is a geometric condensed display with strong vertical proportions and a touch of French poster sensibility. Nunito is a rounded humanist sans, optimized for screen reading at small sizes. The pairing reads as editorial-meets-approachable: display brings authority, body brings comfort. Mono is used scarcely.

### Hierarchy

- **Display** (700, 48px, line-height 1.15, tracking -0.02em): Hero headlines on marketing pages and the few app surfaces that deserve a moment — onboarding success, badge unlock, course completion.
- **Headline** (700, 28px, line-height 1.29, tracking -0.015em): Page-level h1 in product surfaces. Dashboard greeting, parcours title, modal headers.
- **Title** (600, 22px, line-height 1.36, tracking -0.01em): Card titles, section headers. The most-deployed display size in the product.
- **Body Large** (400, 18px, line-height 1.56): Lead paragraphs, intro copy, lesson body text. Caps at 65–75ch.
- **Body** (400, 16px, line-height 1.5): Default copy. Reading-comfort baseline.
- **Body Small** (400, 15px, line-height 1.6): Card descriptions, secondary content within dense layouts.
- **Label** (600, 13px, line-height 1.23, tracking +0.02em): Form labels, badge text, metadata captions.
- **Eyebrow** (700, 11px, line-height 1.45, tracking +0.08em, uppercase): JetBrains Mono micro-label above section headers. The single mono surface in product chrome.
- **Button** (600, 15px, line-height 1.2, tracking -0.01em): All button labels, regardless of size.

### Named Rules

**The League-Spartan-or-Nothing Rule.** Display sizes (≥22px) are always League Spartan, weight 600–700. Nunito at 22px+ feels too round and too soft for the brand's authority register.

**The 65ch Reading Rule.** Body and Body Large copy is capped at 65–75 characters per line. Long-form lesson content sits in a `max-w-prose` container. Dashboards and data surfaces ignore this rule because they are scanning surfaces, not reading surfaces.

**The Mono Eyebrow Rule.** JetBrains Mono appears only in: (1) eyebrow tags above section headers (`COMPÉTENCE 3 SUR 7`), (2) code blocks in lesson content, (3) numeric data labels in dashboards, (4) the `IA` transparency label (see §8). Never in body text, never in buttons, never in display.

## 4. Elevation

The system is **flat-by-default with deliberate glass moments**. Most surfaces sit at level 0 — a card on the page canvas, a button on a card, a chip on a button. Depth is introduced sparingly: a hover lift on interactive cards, a frosted overlay on modals, a tonal shadow on a hovered CTA.

Shadows are **warm-tinted, not neutral grey**. The default `shadow-sm` is a near-neutral with 10% black opacity, but hover states use color-tinted shadow tokens: `shadow-brand-md` is teal-tinted, `shadow-warm-md` is amber-tinted, `shadow-sun-sm` is golden.

### Shadow Vocabulary

- **shadow-xs** (`0 1px 2px 0 rgba(0,0,0,0.05)`): Hairline lift on ghost buttons. Almost invisible.
- **shadow-sm** (`0 1px 3px 0 rgba(0,0,0,0.10), 0 1px 2px -1px rgba(0,0,0,0.10)`): Default card resting state.
- **shadow-md** (`0 4px 6px -1px rgba(0,0,0,0.10), 0 2px 4px -2px rgba(0,0,0,0.10)`): Interactive card hover.
- **shadow-lg** (`0 10px 15px -3px rgba(0,0,0,0.10), 0 4px 6px -4px rgba(0,0,0,0.10)`): Feature cards, elevated dashboards, sticky panels.
- **shadow-xl** (`0 28px 72px -20px rgba(18,24,28,0.22)`): Modal containers, hero cards over photography.
- **shadow-brand-sm / md**: Teal-tinted hover glow on primary CTAs.
- **shadow-warm-sm / md**: Amber-tinted hover glow on secondary CTAs and warm-tinted cards.
- **shadow-sun-sm**: Golden-tinted hover glow on accent CTAs and sun-tinted celebration surfaces.

### Glass Vocabulary

- **glass-light** (`backdrop-blur: 8px`): Subtle frosted overlay on tinted card hovers.
- **glass-medium** (`backdrop-blur: 16px`): Default frosted glass — modals, sticky headers, hero glass cards.
- **glass-heavy** (`backdrop-blur: 24px`): Strong overlays — sidebars over content, drawer panels.
- **glass-ambient** (`blur: 60px`): Decorative gradient blobs in marketing hero backgrounds. The only "decorative" glass; never in product surfaces.

### Named Rules

**The Warm-Tinted Hover Rule.** Hover-state shadows are always tone-tinted, never neutral. A primary button hovers with `shadow-brand-md` (teal-tinted), not `shadow-md` (neutral). Shadow color follows the element's brand role.

**The Glass-as-Signal Rule.** Glass morphism appears only on surfaces that **overlap other content**: modals over a scrim, sticky headers over scrolling content, hero cards over photography, drawer panels over the main canvas. A static card on a flat page never uses glass.

**The Flat Resting Rule.** Cards at rest carry `shadow-sm` at most. Hover lifts add `shadow-md` or a tonal glow, plus a `-1px` translate. Stacked depth (cards inside cards inside cards) is forbidden — flatten the hierarchy instead.

## 5. Components

### Buttons

The button is the system's signature gesture. **Always pill-shaped** (`{rounded.pill}` 999px). Never square, never `rounded-md`, never `rounded-full` (which is a circle for icon-only buttons of equal width and height).

- **Shape:** Pill, 999px border-radius. Height 44px (touch target), padding 0 20px horizontal.
- **Primary** (`button-primary`): Background `{colors.coastal-teal-cta}` #4A8FA1, white text. Default action across the app.
- **Hover:** Background lightens one step to `{colors.coastal-teal}` #55A1B4, with `shadow-brand-md` teal-tinted glow and `-translate-y-0.5` lift.
- **Active / Pressed:** Background deepens to `{colors.coastal-teal-pressed}` #3D7786, translate returns to 0.
- **Secondary / Warm** (`button-secondary`): Background `{colors.warm-amber}` #ED843A, white text. Coaching CTAs, masterclass enrollment, anything signaling human warmth.
- **Accent / Celebration** (`button-accent`): Background `{colors.golden-sun}` #F8B044, white text. Rare — used on JAC ceremony, badge claim, completion CTAs.
- **Ghost** (`button-ghost`): Background `{colors.teal-mist}` #E8F4F7, text `{colors.teal-darkest}`. Secondary action when paired with a primary CTA.
- **Glass / Glass-Light / Glass-Brand:** For specific overlay contexts (saturated dark heroes, light tinted cards).
- **Destructive:** Red-600 background, white text. Used only on irreversible destructive actions (delete account, cancel subscription).
- **Link:** Underlined text in `coastal-teal-pressed`. For inline navigation only.
- **Icon-only:** Square aspect-ratio variant of any of the above. Requires `aria-label`. Width matches height (44px md, 32px sm, 48px lg).

### Cards

The card is the system's primary container. **Always `{rounded.xl}` 20px corners.** Default white surface with a hairline border.

- **Corner Style:** `{rounded.xl}` 20px. Larger cards may use `{rounded.2xl}` 24px (rare).
- **Background:** Default `surface-white`. Tinted variants pull from `teal-mist`, `warm-amber-mist`, `golden-sun-mist` based on the section's tone.
- **Border:** 1px `border-neutral` #e5e7eb at rest. On hover, shifts to `ink-300` or the tonal equivalent.
- **Shadow Strategy:** `shadow-sm` at rest, `shadow-md` (or tone-tinted) on interactive hover. Never stacked shadows.
- **Internal Padding:** `{spacing.stack-lg}` 24px (md, default). Small cards use 16px, large cards 32px.
- **Variants:** `default` · `interactive` · `feature` · `glass` · `glass-brand / warm / dark` · `tinted` · `minimal` · `bordered` · `muted` · `sunken`.
- **Tone-aware:** Cards accept a `tone` prop (`primary` / `warm` / `sun` / `brand`) that recolors title, eyebrow, and hover state. Tone follows the section's narrative role, not the designer's mood.

### Inputs / Fields

- **Shape:** `{rounded.lg}` 14px.
- **Style:** White background, 1px `border-neutral` stroke. No fill.
- **Height:** 44px (touch target). Padding 10px vertical, 14px horizontal.
- **Typography:** Nunito 16px body, ink-deepest text. Placeholder in ink-subtle.
- **Focus:** Border shifts to `coastal-teal` #55A1B4. No glow ring, no inset shadow — just the border swap. Outline appears only on `:focus-visible` at 2px offset.
- **Error:** Border shifts to `danger-coral`, helper text below in same color, with an `AlertCircle` icon. Color is never the sole carrier of meaning.
- **Disabled:** Background shifts to `surface-cool`, text to `ink-subtle`, opacity 0.5.

### Badges & Pills

- **Shape:** `{rounded.pill}` 999px. Always.
- **Padding:** 2px vertical, 10px horizontal.
- **Typography:** Label (Nunito 13px 600, +0.02em tracking).
- **Variants by role:** `brand` · `warm` · `sun` · `success` · `danger` · `neutral`.
- **Status Badge:** Variant with icon prefix — `locked` / `available` / `in-progress` / `completed` / `failed`. Each state has a distinct icon, never color-only.
- **Trending Badge:** Gradient animated promo badge for trending / popular / recommended / featured / new. Used sparingly — reserved for editorial highlights.
- **AI Label** (`ai-label`): Special pill — teal-mist bg, teal-pressed text, JetBrains Mono "IA" eyebrow. Mandatory on every AI output. See §8.

### Navigation

- **Sidebar:** Vertical, 248px wide on desktop, collapsible to icon-only. White background, hairline right border. Items as `NavItem` — 44px height, icon + label, active state in `teal-mist` background with `coastal-teal-cta` icon.
- **Topbar:** 64px height, white background, hairline bottom border. Breadcrumb (left), search (center optional), user menu + notifications (right).
- **Breadcrumb:** Two variants. `simple` for read-only context; `nav` with interactive buttons, ChevronRight separators, ellipsis collapse on overflow.
- **Active state:** Always tonal. Primary nav active uses `teal-mist` + `coastal-teal` icon. Never generic grey.

### Hero Patterns — Pick by Surface, Not by Default

The EditorialHero is **one option among several**. Using it on every page flattens hierarchy — the hero becomes wallpaper, not signal. The selector below maps surface type to hero pattern:

| Surface | Recommended Pattern | Why |
|---------|---------------------|-----|
| Marketing public (`/marketing/*`) | Immersive hero — MeshGradient + parallax + GradientText | Brand surface, motion-rich, attention-grabbing |
| Learner dashboard (`/dashboard`) | **No hero** — compact context bar + action card first | Action-first rule (§9), 3-second answer to « quelle est mon action ? » |
| Detail content (article, masterclass, project detail, parcours detail) | **EditorialHero** | Long-form editorial content earns a hero |
| Lesson player (`/lesson/:id`) | LessonHeader (progress + breadcrumb + reprise) | Surface serves « practice », not « discover » |

## 6. Per-Altitude Design Rules

The system serves four roles, each with a separate UI altitude. **No screen serves two roles at once.** Mixing altitudes destroys decision velocity and produces the schizophrenic cockpit-on-top-neglected-on-bottom design.

### Operational Altitude (Learner, Coach)

The 80% surface. Where the human practices.

- **Single action per viewport.** The dashboard answers "what's my action now?" in under 3 seconds. The lesson player answers "what's the next step in this lesson?" without scrolling.
- **Progress is visible.** Where am I in the parcours, in the mission, in the Dreyfus ladder? Always present, never hidden behind a tab.
- **Practice validation > content completion.** Hierarchy privileges JAC, FAST, EDRA, validated projects. Progress bars of cours are secondary. The Passeport is the source of truth, not the completion percentage.
- **Pause-resume is first-class.** The system retrieves the learner exactly where they were — draft journal saved, lesson player position, mission step in progress. Zero re-onboarding after a vacation, sprint, or parental leave.
- **Density: low to medium.** White space for cognitive breathing room.
- **Motion budget: ≤200ms per interaction.** Tailwind transitions only — no framer-motion in product chrome.

### Strategic Altitude (Manager, CLO)

The 15% surface. Where the L&D pilot conducts the portfolio.

- **Portfolio view, not individual lens.** Cohort flux, capability heatmap, atrophy signals, intervention points.
- **Comparison, filtering, drill-down, export.** Data tables, heatmaps, radars allowed.
- **Trends matter more than snapshots.** A line chart over 6 months beats a giant number. Hero-metric tiles ("87% adoption !") are banned (cf. anti-references in PRODUCT.md).
- **Override is always visible** on AI recommendations destined for managers / admins.
- **Density: medium to high.** Strategic users tolerate density when it earns its weight.
- **Motion budget: ≤150ms.** Sort, filter, expand/collapse. Nothing more.

### Marketing Altitude (Public surface)

The 5% surface. Where prospects encounter the brand.

- **Immersive.** High polish. Animated. Hero-driven.
- **framer-motion primitives allowed** (mesh-gradient, magnetic CTAs, scroll-driven storytelling, parallax). All carry `useReducedMotion()` fallbacks.
- **Density: low.** Editorial pacing.
- **Motion budget: liberal but reduced-motion-safe.**

### Named Rule: The One Altitude Per Viewport Rule

A single screen carries one and only one altitude.

- The Manager dashboard does not embed the learner's lesson player.
- The Learner dashboard does not show the org-wide capability heatmap.
- The Marketing site does not import the product's data-density patterns.
- The Coach hub is operational; the Coach analytics page is strategic. They are separate routes, not tabs in one page.

When in doubt about which altitude a surface serves, ask: *who is the protagonist of this screen, and what verb are they doing?* The verb dictates the altitude.

## 8. AI Transparency Kit

**Doctrine:** the AI augments human practice; it does not replace it. Every AI surface in the product carries a transparent label, sourced rationale, and human override mechanism. The brand rejects magic-copilot framing.

This section operationalizes the 6 IA rules from PRODUCT.md (Doctrine section) and cahier 13bis (GDPR / AI Act / Security).

### Required Patterns on Every AI Output

| Pattern | Implementation | Surface |
|---------|---------------|---------|
| **AI Label** (`ai-label` token) | Pill: teal-mist bg, teal-pressed text, JetBrains Mono "IA" eyebrow. Visible top-right or inline-leading. | Every output — recommendations, summaries, scores, generated content, suggestions |
| **Source Citation** | Hoverable link to the TLS curated corpus chunk. Format: « Source : [titre du contenu, position dans le parcours] » | Chatbot responses, content recommendations, summaries |
| **Confidence Score** | Subtle progress bar (0–100%) or descriptor ("Confiance élevée / moyenne / faible"). Position: below or beside the output. | All inference outputs (matching, scoring, recommendations) |
| **Rationale Access** | Disclosed reason behind a chevron: « Pourquoi cette recommandation ? » → expands to source + factors weighted | All matching, scoring, recommendation surfaces |
| **Override Button** | Always visible, never hidden, on recommendations destined for human decision-makers (coach, manager, admin) | Recommendations to humans only — never on learner-facing reco (the learner is not a decision-maker on AI output, they're the subject of it) |

### The Chatbot Rule

The chatbot responds strictly from TLS curated content. **No web search. No invention. No browsing.** Under a confidence threshold (configurable, default 0.7), it orients the learner to a human coach with the copy: « Je ne suis pas sûr à 100 %. Ton coach [Nom] peut t'aider sur ce point — veux-tu lui en parler ? »

### The Auto-Tagging Ban

Auto-tagging is forbidden (cf. cahier 12bis). The AI **suggests**; a human (Pierre, Admin) **validates**. The suggestion UI is always:

```
[AI suggests: "Compétence: Pilotage Stratégique"]
  └→ [Human approves ✓] OR [Human rejects ✗]
  └→ state persists, audit trail logged
```

### Bias Monitoring Pattern

Every AI decision (matching, scoring, recommendation) is logged with rationale and override events. The audit trail is visible in the Admin BO. Fairness metrics (demographic parity on matching outcomes) are reviewed quarterly.

### Visual Vocabulary — What NOT to Use

- ❌ Purple-cyan gradients ("intelligence" cliché)
- ❌ Sparkle iconography **as decoration** (ambient twinkle in corners, glow trails, "magical" overlays, sparkle confetti around text)
- ❌ "Neural" / "magic" / "copilot" / "AI superpowers" copy
- ❌ Anthropomorphic **chatbot mascotte** (smiling-face character, name like "Atlas / Sextant", avatar with personality, profile picture beside chat messages)
- ❌ **Silent** ghost suggestions in input fields (Copilot-style suggestion injected without explicit user request)
- ❌ Loading spinners labeled "AI is thinking..." (anthropomorphizes the model)
- ❌ Mystery boxes / surprise reveals on AI outputs

### Visual Vocabulary — What TO Use

- ✅ **Instrument framing** — calibrated tool, not assistant
- ✅ **Humble verbs** in copy: « suggère », « recommande », « propose », « calcule »
- ✅ **Sparkles icon as functional marker.** Lucide `Sparkles` is the canonical leading icon on AI-feature buttons (« Positionnement IA », « Suggérer », « Recommander »), on the `IA` label pill, and as eyebrow on AI-generated content blocks. The icon has migrated into industry convention by 2026 — learners associate it with "AI feature here" the way they associate a magnifying glass with search. Refusing it on doctrinal purity would isolate TLS in its own visual dialect. Never decorative, never animated continuously, never combined with purple-cyan fills.
- ✅ **Anthropomorphic logo animation.** The TLS logo mark animates when the system is computing on the learner's behalf (positionnement, matching, generating a recommendation). A subtle 1.2s loop — like a living sign — not a spinner. The brand-as-presence pattern (cf. Claude's pulsing logo) signals « we are working on it » without anthropomorphizing the algorithm itself.
- ✅ **Ghost-typing on AI chat streaming.** When the chatbot streams a response, characters appear with a soft typewriter cadence (~30 char/sec, easeOutQuad rhythm). This is **rendering transparency** — showing the model produces text linearly — not theater. Honors `prefers-reduced-motion` (drops to instant). The chat **never** has an anthropomorphic avatar profile picture; the voice of the message is the `IA` label + the source citation.
- ✅ **Confidence bar + source citation** as the default UI for any output
- ✅ **Visible override** on every recommendation to humans
- ✅ **Audit trail accessible** to admins via standard table UI (not "trust dashboard" theater)

## 9. Cadence Patterns

The learner uses TLS 2-3 sessions per week, 15-40 minutes each, **during work hours**. Not daily. Not at night. Not on weekends. Not without interruption. The design must respect this reality.

This section operationalizes the Cadence Reality from PRODUCT.md.

### Non-Negotiable Rules

**The No-Daily-Streak Rule.** No Duolingo punitive streak. TLS streak is weekly with 1-2 day tolerance (cf. cahier 05: 3-day / 7-day / 30-day tiers, configurable per learner — never 365-day-no-break).

**The Action-First Dashboard Rule.** The learner dashboard opens on the **action of the day**, never on the historical chart. The week journal is secondary, not hero. The Dreyfus radar is on `/passeport`, not on `/dashboard`.

**The Weekend Silence Rule.** Notifications are silent on weekends by default (configurable per learner). No Sunday-evening guilt-trip emails. No Saturday-morning push notifications. No relance dimanche 22h.

**The Pause-Resume Rule.** The system retrieves the learner exactly where they were: draft journal saved, lesson player position, mission step in progress. Zero re-onboarding after a vacation, sprint, parental leave, or burnout absence.

**The Atrophy Calmness Rule.** The 90-day skill atrophy (cf. cahier 05) is signaled by a **calm fade** plus a re-practice suggestion. Never a red alert. Never a guilt message ("Tu as perdu ton niveau !"). The Dreyfus regression is named factually: « Ta compétence X est à rafraîchir » or « Ta compétence X a glissé de Compétent à Avancé ».

### Motion Budget by Altitude

| Altitude | Per-Interaction Budget | Allowed Primitives |
|----------|----------------------|--------------------|
| Operational (learner, coach) | ≤ 200ms | Tailwind transitions (opacity, transform, color, shadow) |
| Strategic (manager, CLO) | ≤ 150ms | Tailwind transitions only (sort, filter, expand/collapse) |
| Marketing (public) | Liberal | framer-motion primitives with `useReducedMotion()` fallback |

### Reduced-Motion Rule

Every animation honors `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`. No exceptions. Marketing primitives all carry the `useReducedMotion()` hook check; product chrome uses CSS `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce) { animation: none; transition: none; }` blanket overrides.

### Notification Pattern

- **In-app only in V1** (no SMS, no push, no email blast for engagement nudges)
- **Maximum 2 per working day**, 0 on weekends (default)
- **Red badges** are reserved for destructive-confirmed errors only — never for engagement nudges
- **Notification copy is calm** (« Une nouvelle suggestion de coach est disponible ») — never urgent (« ⏰ Réponds maintenant ! »)
- **No countdown timers** that force action. The learner sets the tempo.

### Celebration Pattern

When practice is validated (JAC approved, badge earned, parcours completed):
- The badge appears **calmly** — no "!" in copy, no confetti animation by default.
- Confetti is allowed once per learner-lifetime for the first JAC validated, and on rare doctrinal moments (e.g., D5 Diamant Dreyfus reached). Otherwise, a `CelebrationModal` with golden-sun tone + 1 line of warm congrats text suffices.
- Sound is muted by default. Configurable in user preferences.

## 11. Do's and Don'ts

### Do

- **Do** lock the learner as protagonist on every learner-facing screen. The question « quelle est mon action maintenant ? » must answer in under 3 seconds.
- **Do** use one altitude per viewport. Operational OR strategic, never both. Coach correction screen ≠ coach analytics screen.
- **Do** use practice grammar: *reprends*, *valide*, *soumets*, *maîtrise*. Forbidden: *visionne*, *termine*, *complète*.
- **Do** carry one dominant tone per screen, with at most one accent tone. Use the §10 per-flow mapping as the canonical source.
- **Do** label every AI output with the `ai-label` pill, source citation, and confidence score. Override button on recommendations to humans.
- **Do** apply `tu` on learner-personal screens (dashboard, Passeport, journal, lesson player). Apply `vous` everywhere else (coach hub, manager views, admin, marketing, emails, footer).
- **Do** use `{rounded.pill}` 999px for every button. The pill is the brand's signature gesture.
- **Do** use `{colors.coastal-teal-cta}` #4A8FA1 as the default primary fill — not `coastal-teal` #55A1B4 itself (which is the hover state).
- **Do** apply tone-tinted hover shadows (`shadow-brand-md`, `shadow-warm-md`, `shadow-sun-sm`) instead of neutral grey.
- **Do** pair League Spartan display with Nunito body — never the reverse.
- **Do** cap body copy at 65–75ch (`max-w-prose`).
- **Do** reserve glass morphism for surfaces that overlap other content (modals, sticky headers, hero glass).
- **Do** use the `min-h-touch` (44px) utility on every interactive element.
- **Do** use `gap-stack` (16px) and `gap-section` (32px) semantic spacing tokens.
- **Do** respect the cadence: no daily streak, weekend silent by default, pause-resume first-class, atrophie calm.
- **Do** treat the badge unlock calmly — no "!", no confetti by default.

### Don't

- **Don't** mix altitudes in one screen. The CLO dashboard never embeds the learner's lesson player. The learner dashboard never shows the org heatmap.
- **Don't** mix three saturated tones on one screen with equal weight (Christmas-tree page).
- **Don't** use passive consumption verbs (*visionne*, *termine*, *complète*) in product copy.
- **Don't** hide the AI label or skip the source citation. Magic copilot framing is banned by doctrine.
- **Don't** use purple-cyan gradients, sparkle **as decoration**, or anthropomorphic chatbot **mascotte** (no smiling character, no "Atlas / Sextant" persona, no avatar beside chat messages). The Sparkles icon AS functional AI-feature marker is allowed (§13). The TLS logo animating during inference is allowed and signature (§13).
- **Don't** ship aggressive gamification: streak-loss anxiety, urgent red push notifications, Pavlovian XP popups. The brand respects the adult learner.
- **Don't** use `rounded-full` for buttons. That's circle territory — icon-only avatars and dots only.
- **Don't** use the EdTech-cliché `#2196F3` Material Blue, the Duolingo `#58CC02` green, or any other saturated color outside the three brand tones.
- **Don't** use serif fonts. The system is sans-serif end-to-end (League Spartan + Nunito).
- **Don't** use bright Tailwind defaults for semantic colors. `text-red-500` / `bg-green-500` are banned — use `text-danger-fg` / `bg-success-bg`.
- **Don't** stack glass-on-glass, or use glass as decoration on a static card. Glass is signal, not finish.
- **Don't** use border-left or border-right stripes greater than 1px as colored accents.
- **Don't** use gradient text (`background-clip: text` on a gradient) outside the marketing `GradientText` primitive.
- **Don't** use hero-metric SaaS templates (big number + small label + supporting stats + gradient accent). The brand is post-SaaS-cliché.

- **Don't** rely on `style={{}}` for layout, color, or spacing. Reserved for runtime-only values.
- **Don't** use em dashes (—) in UX copy. Use commas, colons, periods.
- **Don't** use exclamation marks in product copy (except destructive-confirmed error).
- **Don't** send notifications on weekends by default.
- **Don't** force urgency via countdown timers or « offer expires » copy.

## 13. Visual Vocabulary — Signature Surfaces

The system has a **positive visual identity** beyond "what we reject". These are the affirmative signatures that make TLS recognizable as TLS — not as a generic warm-light SaaS.

### Signature 1 — Intentional Glassmorphism

Glass is a **brand signature** when used purposefully. It signals **a surface that overlaps content**: modal over a scrim, sticky aside over a scrolling thread, hero card over a tinted background, drawer over the main canvas.

| Pattern | Use |
|---------|-----|
| `glass-light` (8px blur, white/70 bg) | Tinted card hover, subtle inline overlay |
| `glass-medium` (16px blur, white/80 bg) | **Default glass** — modals, sticky headers, hero glass cards |
| `glass-heavy` (24px blur, white/90 bg) | Drawer panels, sidebars over content |
| `glass-ambient` (60px blur, opacity 30%) | Marketing background blobs only |

**Anti-pattern:** glass-on-glass (stacked frosted surfaces), glass on flat static cards (decorative, not signal), glass with high-saturation backgrounds (loses readability).

**Coastal glass.** When tinted, glass takes the **dominant tone of the surface** — teal-tinted on primary surfaces, amber-tinted on coaching, sun-tinted on celebration. Never neutral grey glass. The system rejects the cold "AI SaaS dark glass" mood — TLS glass is warm, daytime, coastal.

### Signature 2 — Diffuse Ambient Gradients

Backgrounds carry **soft tonal gradients** that recede behind content — never compete with it. The `AmbientBlobs` pattern (two or three large blurred radial gradients in tone-mist colors) is the signature TLS background for hero sections, auth surfaces, and editorial pages.

| Surface | Gradient pattern |
|---------|------------------|
| Marketing hero | `MeshGradient` + 2-3 parallax `AmbientBlobs` (medium intensity) |
| Auth pages | `AuthShell` with full-bleed coastal gradient + 1 floating blob |
| Editorial hero | `surface-mist` base + 1 single low-opacity blob in the dominant tone |
| Dashboard / Product chrome | Flat `surface-white` or `surface-cool` — gradients reserved for hero context |
| Modal scrim | Solid `ink-deepest/40` — never gradient |

**Diffusion rules.** Blob blur ≥ 60px. Opacity ≤ 30%. Position **off-center** (top-right, bottom-left) so gradient is asymmetric, never centered. No hard color stops, no radial bullseye, no holographic palette. The mood is « lumière côtière en fin d'après-midi » — coastal-afternoon-light, not synthwave.

### Signature 3 — Sparkles Icon as Functional AI Marker

The Lucide `Sparkles` icon is **allowed and used** as a functional marker for AI features:

- Inline leading icon on AI-feature buttons (« Positionnement IA », « Recommandations », « Suggérer un parcours »)
- Leading icon on the `IA` label pill (combined with the JetBrains Mono « IA » eyebrow)
- Eyebrow icon for AI-generated content blocks (« Suggestion IA » sections in coach hub)

**The Sparkles icon is never:**
- Used decoratively (ambient twinkle in corners, glow trails, "magical" overlays)
- Combined with purple-cyan gradient fills (cliché)
- Animated continuously (the icon is static; if the system is computing, use Signature 4)
- Pluralized as 3+ sparkles around text (sparkle confetti)

The icon convention has **migrated**: by 2026, learners associate sparkles with "AI feature here" the way they associate a magnifying glass with search. Refusing it on doctrinal purity would isolate TLS in its own visual dialect.

### Signature 4 — Anthropomorphic Logo Animation

The TLS logo mark **animates when the system is computing on the learner's behalf** — positionnement, matching, generating a recommendation, streaming a chat response.

The logo behaves like a **living sign**, not a spinner. The pattern signals « we are working on it » **without** anthropomorphizing the algorithm itself.

#### Roadmap — V0 → V1

**V0.5 (current, ships now)** — Orbital cascade on inner shapes.
The TLS mark has 4 SVG paths (`main` halo + `center` dot + `dotTop` + `dotBot`). When `loading={true}`, each shape pulses independently with **staggered delays**, creating a wave that travels through the mark: `main → dotTop → center → dotBot → loop`. Implementation:
- `main` halo: softer amplitude (`scale 0.98 → 1.02`, `opacity 0.72 → 1.0`), 2.2s cycle — anchors the mark without wobbling
- Three dots: full amplitude (`scale 0.92 → 1.08`, `opacity 0.55 → 1.0`), 1.8s cycle
- Cascade delays: `dotTop` +0.20s, `center` +0.42s, `dotBot` +0.64s
- Easing: `cubic-bezier(0.45, 0, 0.55, 1)` (ease-in-out-quad)
- `transform-box: fill-box` + `transform-origin: center` so each path scales from its own visual center
- Pure CSS keyframes scoped inside the `<svg>` via `<style>` tag — no external dependency, no JS animation lib

**Why orbital cascade (not whole-logo rotation):** the 4 shapes are the mark's identity. Animating them individually reads as "the mark is alive, thinking, computing on your behalf" — closer to Claude's loading mark. Rotating the whole bubble/SVG reads as a generic spinner. Geometry preserved on each path; only `transform: scale` + `opacity` animate.

**V1 (target, ships with brand refresh)** — Shape-morphing logo.
The logo silhouette morphs between **2–3 brand-validated states** on a 1.6–2.0s loop, like Claude's loading mark (path interpolation between distinct shapes). Each shape is a deliberately-designed variation of the TLS mark, not a random distortion. Requires:
- 2–3 SVG silhouettes validated by the brand team (Canva brand kit + Notion brand guidelines)
- `framer-motion` `<motion.path>` with `d` attribute interpolation via the [flubber](https://github.com/veltman/flubber) interpolator, or `<motion.svg>` morphing between `<path>` keyframes
- Easing: `ease-in-out-quad` (no overshoot, no bounce)
- Reduced-motion fallback: V0 pulse (no morph)

**Why defer to brand refresh:** the morph silhouettes are **brand assets**, not engineering decisions. Designing them requires the brand team's input on what shapes the TLS mark is "allowed" to take. Implementing V1 before brand validation produces arbitrary morph paths that fail brand review.

#### State table (applies to both V0 and V1)

| State | Pattern |
|-------|---------|
| Idle | Static logo (no animation) |
| Computing | Animation loop active (V0 pulse, V1 morph) |
| Streaming chat | Logo idle + ghost-typing on the message body (Signature 5) |
| Completed | Logo returns to idle, output appears with `motion.fadeIn` 200ms |

**Anti-pattern:** spinning wheels, "thinking" spinners with labels, anthropomorphic mascotte avatars (smiling face), logo distorted into **arbitrary** shapes (V1 morphs must use validated brand silhouettes only).

**Reduced-motion:** all 4 shapes drop to a synchronous opacity breath (1.0 → 0.7 → 1.0 on 1.6s ease-in-out, no transforms, no stagger), accompanied by an `ai-label` pill with text « Calcul en cours ». Never a flashing label. Cascade: V1 morph → V0.5 orbital → V0 opacity breath (reduced-motion) → static + label.

### Signature 5 — Ghost-Typing on AI Streaming

When the chatbot streams a response, characters appear with a soft **typewriter cadence** (~30 char/sec, easeOutQuad rhythm — not linear). The cursor element is a single soft-tinted vertical line, blinking at 1.0s intervals.

**Implementation.** The streaming endpoint emits character chunks; the renderer animates them in via `motion.span` with stagger ≤ 16ms. The animation is **rendering transparency** (showing the model produces text linearly) — not theater (no fake delay added on top of real latency).

**Reduced-motion.** Characters appear in full instantly; cursor is hidden. The output is identical, only the cadence differs.

**Anti-pattern.** Linear typing (mechanical typewriter rhythm), bouncing dots ("..."), avatar profile picture beside the message (we don't have a chatbot mascotte). The voice of the message is the `IA` label + the source citation — not an anthropomorphic presence.

### Signature 6 — Tone-Aware Surfaces

Cards, buttons, headers, eyebrows, and shadows accept a `tone` prop that recolors them along the §10 per-flow mapping. The surface **inherits the narrative role** of its flow:

| Tone | Surface role | Color family |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| `primary` | Practice / progress / browse | Coastal teal |
| `warm` | Coaching / human contact | Amber |
| `sun` | Validation / celebration | Golden |
| `brand` | Assertive moment | Saturated teal-dark |
| `neutral` | Utility / settings | Greyscale only |

The tone propagates to: card border-hover, button gradient, shadow tint, eyebrow color, badge fill. It **does not** propagate to body text (always `ink-body`) or background canvas (always `surface-white`).

## Roadmap — Premium Excellence Gaps

A second-altitude critique surfaces seven gaps where TLS could move from "tasteful French EdTech" to "category-defining." Captured here for the ongoing Phase 19 design excellence pass — partially addressed, partially in roadmap.

### 1. The display system is conservative

League Spartan 700 + tracking -0.02em is correct but tame. Linear and Vercel push display tracking to -3.0px at 80px (≈4% of size); TLS currently caps at -0.02em (≈2%). **Action:** test `tracking-[-0.04em]` on Display and Headline tokens; reserve the tightest tracking for sizes ≥48px. *Status: ✅ Phase 19.D — tracking-display (-0.03em) applied to EditorialHero h1, SectionHeader lg, Card title lg. tracking-headline (-0.025em) for md/sm. Headings across 100+ pages now noticeably tighter.*

### 2. No dark mode and no doctrinal choice not to have one

The system runs light-only by inheritance, not by decision. **Action:** commit to light-only with the doctrine *"The Daytime Atelier Rule — the brand is afternoon coastal light, never night"* OR design a proper dark mode for sleep/journal contexts. The current ambiguity is a category reflex. *Status: light-only locked implicitly; doctrine to write.*

### 3. The illustration system is absent

Headspace, Duolingo, and Notion all carry a signature illustration language. TLS uses Lucide icons (excellent for chrome) but has no custom illustration vocabulary for empty states, onboarding moments, or celebration screens. **Action:** commission or define a 4-shape geometric illustration grammar in the coastal teal + amber + sun palette. **Anti-pattern explicit:** no mascotte, no cute character, no anthropomorphic learning-buddy. *Status: deferred — explicit anti-mascotte rule confirmed.*

### 4. Motion lives only in the marketing site

framer-motion is wired for `/marketing/*` (9 primitives) but the product surface has only Tailwind transitions. **Action:** define 3–5 product-safe motion primitives — `StaggerList` for dashboard sections, `CelebrateOnce` for badge unlocks (rare, doctrinal), `ProgressMorph` for the parcours progression ring. Motion budget ≤200ms per interaction. *Status: not started — Phase 19.x candidate.*

### 5. The shadow vocabulary is generic at rest

The shadow scale (`shadow-xs` → `shadow-xl`) uses standard black-with-opacity drops. Tone-tinted shadows exist but are reactive (hover-only). **Action:** define a resting-state warm shadow — every card at rest carries a faint amber-tinted shadow rather than neutral black-10%. *Status: ✅ Phase 19.D — `--shadow-card` (amber 7% + black 5%), `--shadow-card-hover`, `--shadow-card-lift` tokens added. Card.tsx default/feature/elevated/interactive use shadow-card family. Subtle warmth at rest, visible in DevTools: `rgba(237, 132, 58, 0.07)`.*

### 6. The component library has 51 ui + 40 patterns = 91 names

Headspace ships ~12 core components. Linear's documented set is ~20. **Action:** Phase 19 consolidation pass — collapse `Pill` / `MetaPill` / `Tag` / `FilterChip` decisions into a doctrine table; archive truly-deprecated files; aim for ≤60 component surfaces total. *Status: ✅ Phase 19.A — Chip.tsx primitive extraced (shared CHIP_BASE/SIZE/TONE tokens). 4 wrappers refactored. Single doctrine table in CLAUDE.md §"Famille Pills". AuthFeature removed (Phase 19.C). HeroSection retired (Phase 19.B). Consolidated from ~91 → ~82 named surfaces.*

### 7. No signature interaction

Linear has the command palette. Notion has slash commands. Duolingo has the streak flame. Headspace has the breathing circle. TLS candidates: the parcours progress ring as persistent dashboard widget; the journal "moment marker" timeline; the competence-evidence linker as hover-revealed graph; the Dreyfus radar morphing on mastery progression. **Action:** pick one and make it the brand's UX signature. *Status: candidate identified (Dreyfus radar with atrophy-fade animation), to scope in Phase 19.x.*
